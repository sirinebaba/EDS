Economic Development Solutions

Working hand in hand with economic development actors, our mission is to provide academic-level quality research and consulting solutions on issues we care deeply about.

Why Choose Us

Economic Development Solutions (EDS) is a consulting, data collection, impact assessment, and research firm based in Beirut. Our team is comprised of expert economists, political scientists, urban and environmental specialists, trained qualitative and quantitative data collectors, and field coordinators. Our interdisciplinary nature as a team enables us to take on a variety of projects we are passionate about: agricultural sustainability and eco-management, youth resilience, gender equality and feminism, local economic development, impact-driven evaluations, and livelihood assessments in conflict-affected environments. Since EDS's establishment in January 2017, we have worked on a variety of both countrywide and regional projects encompassing data collection, monitoring, and policy consultancy.

Certainly, EDS’s policy aims to consistently incorporate contextual and gender analysis, focusing on the community and the people understudy as the key providers of knowledge for effective understanding. We pride ourselves on our multilayered and profound understanding of the MENA region's sociopolitical and economic context, while acknowledging that there is always room for further growth.

What We Offer

Impact-driven evaluation

Our team conducts evaluations for local and international organizations, assessing a project’s efficacy, efficiency, relevance, influence and sustainability. We have worked with Save the Children, Mouvement Sociale, Mercy Corps, and the Safadi Foundation on impact-driven project evaluations.

Academic research

We provide thorough and theoretically sound research for universities, think tanks and independent research institutes. Some of our research projects have looked at social and economic sectarianism in Lebanon, the changes in Lebanon's agriculture sector after the 2011 Syrian refugee crisis, and food security in the region.

Gender and community-based analysis

Incorporating gender analysis is a key aspect in our consultation and research. We also believe that engaging with community members and assessing their perspectives are critical for any rigorous findings and recommendations. We have worked with the Lebanese Reforestation Initiative to provide a gender analysis of Lebanon’s forestry sector.

Policy briefings

Given the critical and sensitive nature of the research we conduct, we are keen to provide data and recommendations for sustainable solutions. Through these briefs, our aim is to influence broader policy-making decisions.

Data collection

We collect both qualitative and quantitative data for a diverse range of organizations and clients. Our team's extensive and detail-oriented experience in data collection ensures its capacity to mainstream the Do No Harm approach, in addition to standardizing research ethics (particularly non-discriminatory participation, free consent, and confidentiality). Our field staff are also highly trained in communication techniques that are essential in context-sensitive areas. Some relevant projects include data compilation for Plan International NGO and Monash University’s “Safer Cities for Girls” project and the World Food Programme's Cash Assistance Programme.

Labour market assessments

We conduct market-focused research where we evaluate the needs, opportunities, obstacles, and threats experienced by specific communities in Lebanon and the region. We assess deficits in labor skills within the market and propose methods in which they can be improved, such as vocational training and education, business opportunities within the private sector, policy changes, and community involvement. Our team has worked with the ILO, NRC, and Care International on labor, market-based and/or livelihood assessments.

Our Team

Ilina Srour

PhD
Ilina Srour is an expert in socioeconomic research with more than ten years of experience in the field of applied economics. Throughout her career, she has consulted for national, international and government agencies that are working in the sphere of social and economic development in Lebanon. A list of her clientele includes UNDP, Oxfam, UNIDO, ILO, ESCWA, FAO, NRC, Heinrich Boll, Tripoli Special Economic Zone, the Ministries of Industry, Labor and Agriculture, among others. She has also performed the socio-economic analysis and a financial feasibility study related to the establishment of three industrial zones in Lebanon. She is currently leading the evaluation of DFID’s Lebanon Enterprise Development Program for supporting SME development and job creation. She holds a PhD in economics from the Catholic University of Milan, Italy. Her other research interests include urban economics, poverty, livelihoods, and economics of innovation.
